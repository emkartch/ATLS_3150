Universal Design Overview

Universal Design is a design concept that aims to design devices, environments, and services to be accessible for as many people as possible, regardless of their abilities. Universal Design asks the designer to consider a diverse range of user abilities from the start of the design process, ensuring inclusivity throughout.

7 key principles of Universal Design:

  1. Equitable Use
  2. Flexibility in Use
  3. Simple and Intuitive Use
  4. Perceptible Information
  5. Tolerance for Error
  6. Low Physical Effort
  7. Size and Space for Approach and Use

Goals of Universal Design:

  • Create inclusivity and usability for users with a variety of abilities.
  • Get rid of the need to retrofit accessibility features.
  • Enhance user experience.

Universal Design benefits everyone, including people with temporary or situational limitations, by promoting a more inclusive and accessible environment.
